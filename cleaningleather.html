How to Clean Leather Shoes


Step 1: Prep your shoes for cleaning

Remove the laces from your shoes. Get a towel and warm water with dish soap. Additionally, you could use a Mr. Clean magic eraser or brush. You may also need to use an iron press to remove creases.
Step 2: Wipe down your shoes Wipe off any excess dirt from your shoe with a towel. Then, use the magic eraser to wipe down the entire shoe. Be sure to dip it in soapy water. Leather shoes are easier to clean because they are made out of leather; it's easier to wipe of dirt because the leather does not get stained like canvas.
Step 3: Cleaning the laces Mix a little bleach into the solution and drop the laces in for about 30 minutes to an hour. If your laces are any other color besides white, do not mix bleach with the laces. Instead, just soak them in warm water with dish soap. Let the shoe and the laces dry thorougly before going on to the next step.
Step 4: Removing Creases Leather is a material that can crease over time. Depending on the type of shoe, it may crease quicker than other kinds of shoes. To remvoe creases, soak a towel with warm water and put it on the shoe. Then, use an iron and press over the shoe. This should remove creases if done correctly.
Step 5: Relacing your shoes Make sure everything has dried before moving on to this step. Pull the tongue of your shoe in place and then (starting from the bottom) relace your shoes. You can relace them the same way they came out of the box (factory laced) or customize them however you'd like. Depending on the style you are lacing, pay attention to the tongue and its placement in the shoe.

Keep in mind:
1. Leather can crease, be gentle
2. Use bleach only for white laces
3. Make sure to let the shoes dry completely before lacing them
